Goals & guardrail

Goals & Metrics

A Goal states the outcome — what success looks like; a Metric measures progress toward it. AARRR ("Pirate Metrics" — Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue) maps a customer journey to the right metric at each stage; HEART is the alternative, for iterating on an existing feature's quality and engagement. The Guardrail is a check on the goal, ensuring that there is no negative impact to improving the metric.

The real problem

Identifying the Problem

Job Stories (Jobs to Be Done) reframe a feature ask as the real underlying need — not "add a checkout toggle" but the actual problem it solves. Once problems are named, the Opportunity quadrant scores each one on Customer Signal (how strong the problem is for users) against Business Alignment (how much solving it advances the goal) — only the top-right corner is safe to build without question.
