What the decision hinges on

Two questions decide this — neither is answered yet.

?

If Dragonpass: what do we add?

Assurant already sells this exact route — same platform, same products. No differentiation.

Dragonpass sits behind several of our other lifestyle products already. What stops a partner going to them direct?

We need pricing (no rate card yet) and a clear answer on white-label depth (Android currently requires the Dragonpass app).

?

If Wholesale: can we deliver it?

Full build: API integration, pricing/usage risk, regulatory, support — with IT organisation involvement.

Real financial commitments: guaranteed monthly spend for the best rates; prepay funding.

Honest question: do we have the resource to do this properly, and on what timeline?

Supporting detail

ePay

Green-only zone product (34 European countries), single-use — no top-ups; customer buys a new eSIM each time.

£8.20 = actual Feb 2026 blended EMEA 3GB offer. 5GB-equivalent (~£11.50) derived from our own "5GB ≈ 140% of 3GB" guidance to NatWest — not a quoted ePay price.

Dragonpass

No rate card shared yet. Their steer: launch on pre-packaged rates, move to usage-based once real data exists.

Android journey currently requires the Dragonpass app.

eSIM Go

Spain 5GB/30d: $3.55 (Gold) / $3.72 (Silver) / $4.11 (Standard), + $0.30 assignment + $0.20/mo active-IMSI fee.

Prepay, $1,000 minimum top-up. Best pricing tiers require guaranteed spend: $25k/mo (Silver) / $100k/mo (Gold), reviewed quarterly.

1Global

~€3.85 for 5GB Europe incl. €0.60 one-time provisioning ("one eSIM for life" — reusable across trips).

Consumption billing: charged on actual usage (~65% observed) — breakage accrues to Likewize.

Deposit ~1 month's traffic, min €20k, refundable. Two models: 1Global as Telco of Record (agent — they hold licences/VAT) vs. Likewize as Telco of Record (full wholesale — we hold telecom licences, multi-country VAT).

Assumptions: Currency: $1 = £0.76, €1 = £0.85. Likewize markup held constant across all routes at 20% + £0.04 platform fee (per the original NatWest proposal). 1Global's 65% consumption figure is their number, not independently verified.

Who it's for

Empathising with Users

Personas and Empathy Maps put a face on who we're building for — not to invent detail for its own sake, but to force real focus on someone's context and concerns before designing a solution. A Persona captures who the person is; an Empathy Map digs into what they think, feel, say, and do in the moment that matters, surfacing the Pain that blocks them and the Gain that would actually convert them.

Both personas are hypothetical/generated research — no real customer interviews behind them yet.

Dan

Mid-30s · NatWest current account holder · UK

ArrivalTravels abroad once a year for a main holiday (10–14 days, mainstream European destination), plus maybe one short city break every year or two.

Think & FeelValues simplicity and reliability over lowest price — trusts his bank more than hunting for the cheapest deal.

PainBill-shock fear, not knowing what package he needs, fiddly QR/APN setup, no idea how to top up mid-trip.

GainSeamless connectivity from landing, no unexpected costs. Success: "I didn't have to think about it, and it worked."

Meera

Late-30s · premium packaged bank account holder · UK

ArrivalTravels internationally 6–8 times a year, mixed work and personal, evaluating/holding a bundle with travel insurance, phone insurance and lounge access.

Think & FeelValues genuine value-for-money on bundles — does the maths on separate costs vs. one packaged fee, expects what she pays for to work automatically.

PainDoing "sort my data" repeatedly every trip; the bundle breaking its no-thought promise if manual activation is still needed; running out of allowance or hitting an uncovered destination.

GainConnectivity as one less thing to manage. Success: never thinking about data at all, because it's already sorted.

The real problem

Identifying the Problem

Job Stories (Jobs to Be Done) reframe a feature ask as the real underlying need — not "add a checkout toggle" but the actual problem it solves. Once problems are named, the Opportunity quadrant scores each one on Customer Signal (how strong the problem is for users) against Business Alignment (how much solving it advances the goal) — only the top-right corner is safe to build without question.

Job stories

The ask named a fix (add eSIM), not a problem — one level down, the real needs are:

Unmet · Dan

When I'm in the final days before a trip abroad, I want a simple, trustworthy way to sort my mobile data without researching options or risking a surprise bill, so I can travel with one less thing to worry about.

Unrealized · Meera

When I'm choosing or reviewing my premium bank account, I want to know my everyday travel connectivity is already covered without extra effort, so I can trust the bundle is genuinely paying for itself, trip after trip.

Unrealized · Partner-level, kept for context

When a partner like NatWest is evaluating how to compete on customer travel benefits, they want a flexible, commercially competitive eSIM proposition they can offer under their own brand, so they can differentiate without taking on connectivity operational risk themselves.
